## Context

Omega Test House is a SANS / SABS / SA Watermark-accredited test laboratory in South Africa — they certify plumbing components against South African National Standards. Compliance means the products on the shelf actually meet the standard. Non-compliance is a measurable risk to consumers and a legal exposure for suppliers.

I built the test rigs Omega uses to run tests against a SANS certified standard.

## What I built

Custom industrial test equipment for durability testing SANS-1480 & SANS-226 of plumbing fittings — taps, valves, drains, traps. Each rig is a physical apparatus (aluminium extrusion frames, plumbing manifolds, actuators) controlled by custom electronics and software I designed and wrote.

- **Mechanical** — aluminium extrusion frames sized to each standard's test specs, custom fixtures for each fitting class.
- **Electronics** — sensor arrays (flow, pressure, temperature, cycle counters, torque), motor and valve drivers, control boards.
- **Firmware & control software** — Python and C / C++ running the cycle programmes called for by each SANS standard, logging every measurement for the audit trail.

## Why it matters

Test laboratories live and die on the reproducibility of their methods. The rigs need to run the same cycle, the same way, every time, for years — that's what makes a "test report" trustworthy. The hardest part isn't building *one* rig; it's building rigs that hold their tolerances over thousands of cycles and produce defensible data.
