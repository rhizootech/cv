## Context

Think Digital operates the captive-portal ad network across free public WiFi access points in South Africa — shopping centres, restaurants, transport hubs. The business sells time-on-portal to advertisers; the engineering challenge is *fairness and accuracy* across thousands of access points, hundreds of thousands of devices, and many concurrent campaigns running at different price points and run lengths.

I joined as a contractor through RCT in 2022 and stayed until the end of 2024 — three years of building and running the platform that powers the ad network.

## What I built

### 1. The ad-pacing algorithm

Every campaign has an insertion order: a number of impressions to deliver, a start date, an end date. Naive delivery (max throughput, first-come-first-served) burns the budget early and leaves campaigns stranded. The pacing algorithm spreads delivery across the run so each campaign hits its IO smoothly and on time — accounting for access-point traffic patterns, day-of-week effects, and concurrent campaigns competing for the same audience.

### 2. MAC-keyed client context

Showing the same ad to the same device over and over is the fastest way to destroy a captive-portal network's value. The system keys client context off WiFi MAC addresses, so device-level rotation prevents the ad-fatigue that drives users away from the portal entirely.

### 3. Pub/Sub → BigQuery event pipeline

Every captive-portal interaction — click, scroll, dwell, dismiss — flows through Google Pub/Sub into BigQuery. That dataset powers operational reports, revenue reports, and the analytics back-office partners use to track their campaigns.

### 4. Django back-office

A full management plane for the people who run the business:

- **Sales partners** create and manage campaigns
- **Management partners** track their portfolios and revenue
- **Think Digital management and employees** track sales, manage the access-point fleet, and generate reports

## Stack & architecture

Python, Django, DRF for the back-office and APIs. PostgreSQL for transactional data. Google Pub/Sub for event ingestion. Google Cloud Functions to update pacing for each AP in the Ad Delivery network. Google BigQuery for the analytics warehouse. JavaScript for the captive-portal client surface and a small amount of in-app interactivity.

## Outcome

The engagement ended amicably at the close of 2024. The platform was the core operational system of the business — the pacing algorithm, the event pipeline, the back-office — running production load every day across South Africa for two and a half years.
